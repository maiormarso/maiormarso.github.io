Wire Cage by a JarHead
Once in a while we went to take a shower
Had to walk over the hill took an hour
On the way back came across a wire cage
With a man inside to old for his age

He was a prisoner in his own land
Crouched inside bent over he could not stand
At this point I was just an observer
I sat down and watched him with great fervor

Threw piece of candy at him from afar
He just looked at me held on to the bar
Watched his jaw slowly moving as he chewed
I left him there calm in his solitude

Became the victim of his own surmise
He just did not know how to throw the dice

Sonnet by Ez Mula 9/2012 - 6/2017.
