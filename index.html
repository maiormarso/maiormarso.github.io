Bee Coloney Decline Claim In the United States





Login at Data.World to view the Bee Colony Dataset

My premise is that Bees are not in a decline, and are maintaining a steady rate of growth despite many obstacles. According to many studies, it has been determined that a high rate of bees dying off, have risen since 2006. up until then, the average loss was 10 percent a year, now it is up to 30 percent. Some say that the bees are still on a decline and put the blame on a variety of reasons. One of reason for Bee decline, is a mite in the hive, which bytes the bee leaving an open wound vulnerable to disease, and even though this article is only using data for the United States, it affects the whole world. Another issue are pesticides, which are introduce into the growth factor of the plant and create foliage, which is detrimental to the bee. Others say that Bees are dying off because bees do not have a variety of plants to obtain the nourishment they require; since bees are rented, then transported to various orchards and fields that only grow a certain crop. According to the dataset used for this article, many states bees actually spend most of their time out of thier own state. Reading the ‘Current Biology’(1), ‘Promiscuous Honey Bee Queens increase colony productivity and suppressing worker selfishness by being multiply inseminated. One might attribute this to the rise in bees in 2012 according to the data collected in this study. The dataset ‘Bee Colony Loss', came from Data,World.com and is available to the general public. It gives the percentage of bee colony loss in each one of the United States even though there are stats that have very few colonies to speak of. It is clear that the bee keepers reporting of their losses cannot be denied and according to the data, the bees are in decline.
We must therefore reject the premise that the bee colonies are not in decline.
